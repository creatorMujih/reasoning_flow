Math problem: write the modulus of 22 % 4

**Prompt:** Write the modulus of 22 % 4

**AI's Response:**
The modulus of 22 % 4 means finding the remainder when 22 is divided by 4.
When you divide 22 by 4:
 22 ÷ 4 = 5 (with a remainder of 2)
So, 22 % 4 = 2.


## Prompt the model: "Let's think step by step before answering."

**Prompt:**

Write the modulus of 22 % 4. Let's think step by step before answering.

**AI's Response:**

1. **What is the modulus operation?**
The modulus (denoted as %) gives you the remainder after dividing one number by another.
For example, 22 % 4 means: What is the remainder when 22 is divided by 4?

2. **Start with division**:
    We begin by dividing 22 by 4:
      22 ÷ 4 = 5.5
      But, we only care about the whole number part of the result for division. So, we take 5 as the quotient.

3. **Find the product of the quotient and divisor**:
   Multiply the quotient (5) by the divisor (4) to see how much 4 fits into 22 without going over.
      5 × 4 = 20

4. **Subtract the product from the original number**:
    Now, subtract 20 (the product of 5 and 4) from 22 (the original number):
        22 - 20 = 2

5. **The result is the remainder**:
    This remainder, 2, is what we get after dividing 22 by 4. The modulus of 22 % 4 is 2.

   **Visualizing the Process:**
    Imagine dividing 22 by 4:
    4 fits into 22 five times, giving us 20 (because 4 × 5 = 20).
    After reaching 20, we have 2 left over (because 22 - 20 = 2).

**Final Answer:**
The remainder after dividing 22 by 4 is 2, so the modulus of 22 % 4 is 2.
